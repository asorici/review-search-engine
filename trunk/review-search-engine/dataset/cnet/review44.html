• 4.0 stars

    "Amazing compact digicam" on February 20, 2010 by pierre53

    Pros: Fast f2.4 lens
    24 mm wide angle zoom
    Extremely fast burst mode, low shutter lag in other modes
    High-quality lens
    HD video in .mp4 format (included software can easily convert to .wmv)
    Astounding low-light handheld "twilight" mode
    Metal tripod mount

    Cons: No direct manual control of aperture or shutter speed, Program mode with no shift but does offer choice of ISO, exposure compensation, flash mode, burst mode, metering mode, focus mode, white balance, bracketing interval, etc.

    Summary: Excellent little camera. After using this camera, I think that, despite not having manual control of aperture and shutter speed, it might be more suitable for photographers who can appreciate and make use of the wider angle and the faster lens. If you're not going to be taking advantage of its low light abilities, a lesser camera in the Sony line would save you money. Otherwise, it's a wonderful pocket camera for photographers who might also have an SLR. I've been especially impressed with its low light abilities, and a 24 mm wide angle opens up a lot more possibilites for creative photographers than does the typical 28 mm. It's great for night time street photography.

    Updated on Feb 21, 2010

    If you get this camera, you can get started quickly using the included printed manual, but for best result beyond just pointing and shooting, I highly recommend reading through the Handbook, which you must install separately from the software on the included CD. It goes into more detail about the various modes and functions. Want to know when to use Motion Blur mode as opposed to Handheld Twilight mode, or how to set your own white balance manually, the Handbook is the place to look.
